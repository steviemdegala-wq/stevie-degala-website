## Home Loan Options for Windsor CO Buyers

One of the biggest advantages of working with a mortgage broker in Windsor CO is the range of loan programs you can access. Here is a plain look at what is available to most buyers in Weld County.

**Conventional Loans.** This is the most popular loan type. You put down as little as three percent, and if your credit score is around 620 or higher, you can qualify for strong rates. Conventional loans work great for buyers with stable income and solid credit history.

**FHA Loans.** The Federal Housing Administration backs these loans so lenders can approve buyers with lower credit scores and smaller down payments. You can qualify with a score as low as 580 and as little as three and a half percent down. If your credit has had some challenges, FHA is often the right starting point.

**VA Loans.** If you served in the military, you may qualify for a VA loan. Zero down payment, no private mortgage insurance, and some of the most competitive rates available anywhere. It is one of the strongest financial benefits available to veterans and active duty service members, and I make sure every eligible buyer knows about it.

**USDA Loans.** Certain areas of Weld County qualify for USDA rural development financing. These loans also offer zero down payment. If the home you want falls in an eligible area, this program could save you thousands of dollars upfront. I can check eligibility for you right away.

**Conventional Jumbo Loans.** Home prices in some Windsor neighborhoods push past the standard conforming loan limits. Jumbo loans cover those higher purchase prices. The qualifying standards are stricter, but the right lender makes it manageable.

**DSCR Loans.** Are you buying a rental property in Windsor? A debt service coverage ratio loan, which qualifies you based on the rental income the property generates rather than your personal income, is a great tool for investors. Windsor's rental market is healthy, and DSCR loans are a smart path for buyers adding to a portfolio.

Every buyer has a different situation. My job is to tell you honestly which loan fits yours and why.

Not exactly. A loan officer at a bank works for that bank and can only offer that bank's products. A mortgage broker like me works for you and has access to many different lenders. That usually means more options and better terms for your situation.

**How much does it cost to use a mortgage broker in Windsor CO?**

In most cases, you pay nothing out of pocket to use a mortgage broker. Lenders pay a fee to the broker when your loan closes. I will always be upfront about how I am compensated so you have no surprises at closing.
