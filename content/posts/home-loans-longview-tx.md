## The Most Common Home Loan Types in Longview

Here is a plain look at the loan programs local buyers use most often.

**Conventional Loans**

A conventional loan is not backed by the government. It is offered through private lenders and usually requires a credit score of at least 620. If you can put 20 percent down, you avoid private mortgage insurance, which is an extra monthly cost that protects the lender if you stop making payments. Conventional loans work well for buyers who have solid credit and some savings set aside.

**FHA Loans**

An FHA loan is backed by the Federal Housing Administration. It allows a down payment as low as 3.5 percent, which is great if you have not had a chance to save a large amount of cash. Credit score requirements are also more flexible. Many first time buyers in Longview start here.

**VA Loans**

If you served in the military, a VA loan might be your best option. These loans are backed by the Department of Veterans Affairs and often require zero down payment. There is no private mortgage insurance either. Veterans and active duty service members in the Longview area use VA loans every day, and they are one of the best deals in the mortgage world.

**USDA Loans**

Parts of East Texas qualify for USDA loans, which are designed for rural and semi rural areas. These loans can also allow zero down payment if the home and buyer both qualify. Some areas near Longview might be eligible, so it is worth asking about when you talk to a broker.

## How Much Do You Need to Buy a Home in Longview TX

This is the question most buyers ask first. The answer depends on your loan type and the price of the home you want.

For an FHA loan on a $200,000 home, your down payment could be as low as $7,000. Closing costs on top of that usually run between 2 and 5 percent of the loan amount, so budget an extra $4,000 to $10,000 for those.

For a conventional loan, your down payment can be as low as 3 percent, though lenders often offer better interest rates when you put more down.

One thing many buyers miss is the seller concession. In a slower market, some sellers will agree to pay part of your closing costs. That can make a big difference in how much cash you need on day one.

## Getting Approved for a Home Loan in Longview

Approval comes down to three main things: your credit score, your income, and your debts.

Lenders want to see that you have enough income to cover your mortgage payment plus your other monthly bills. They calculate your debt to income ratio by adding up all your monthly debt payments and dividing by your gross monthly income. Most loan programs want that number to fall below 43 to 50 percent.

Your credit score tells lenders how reliably you pay back money you borrow. A score above 700 opens more options and better rates. A score in the 600s can still work, especially with FHA or VA loans.

If your credit or debt situation needs some attention, a good mortgage broker can help you build a plan before you apply. Sometimes a few small changes can move you into a much better loan program and save you real money every month.

## FAQ: Home Loans in Longview TX

**What credit score do I need to get a home loan in Longview?**

It depends on the loan type. FHA loans can work with scores as low as 580. Conventional loans usually want 620 or higher. VA loans also have flexible requirements. If your score is below 580, talk to a broker about what steps to take first.

**Can I buy a home in Longview with no down payment?**

Yes, in some cases. VA loans and USDA loans can both allow zero down payment if you qualify. VA loans are available to veterans and active military. USDA loans depend on whether the property location meets rural eligibility guidelines.

**How long does it take to get approved for a home loan?**

Pre approval usually takes one to three business days once you submit your documents. The full loan process from accepted offer to closing typically takes 21 to 45 days depending on the loan type and lender.

**What is the difference between a mortgage broker and a bank?**

A bank can only offer their own loan products. A mortgage broker works with multiple lenders and shops around for better rates and terms on your behalf. That extra comparison can save you a significant amount over the life of your loan.

**Are home prices in Longview TX still affordable?**

Longview remains one of the more affordable markets in Texas. Compared to Dallas or Austin, you get much more home for your money here. Gregg County has seen steady demand, which keeps values stable without the extreme price spikes seen in larger metro areas.
