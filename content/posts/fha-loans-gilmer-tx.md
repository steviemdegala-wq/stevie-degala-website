## What Is an FHA Loan?

An FHA loan is a home loan backed by the federal government. That backing gives lenders more confidence, so they can offer you better terms even if your credit score is not perfect. You do not need a 20 percent down payment like you might with a conventional loan. With an FHA loan, you may qualify with as little as 3.5 percent down if your credit score is 580 or higher. If your score is between 500 and 579, you may still qualify with a 10 percent down payment.

FHA loans also tend to have lower interest rates than some other options, which can save you a good amount of money over the life of your loan.

## FHA Loan Requirements You Need to Know

Here is a plain look at what you generally need to qualify.

A credit score of at least 580 gets you the 3.5 percent down payment option. Scores between 500 and 579 may still qualify with 10 percent down. You will need a steady work history, usually two years with the same employer or in the same field. Your debt load matters too. Lenders look at something called a debt to income ratio, which simply means your total monthly debt payments divided by your monthly income. Most FHA lenders want that number at 43 percent or lower. The home also has to be your primary residence. You cannot use an FHA loan to buy a rental property or a vacation home.

You will also pay a mortgage insurance premium each month. This is a small fee that protects the lender if you ever stop making payments. It is a standard part of FHA loans and is part of why lenders can offer them with smaller down payments.

## Rural and Older Properties in Upshur County

One question that comes up a lot in Gilmer is whether FHA loans work for older homes or properties with a lot of land. The good news is that FHA loans are pretty flexible here. You can finance a home with acreage as long as the land is considered typical for the area and not a large commercial farm.

For older homes, the main thing is that the property has to be livable and safe. If a home needs a lot of work before it meets those standards, there is a special version of the FHA loan called the 203k loan. This lets you roll both the purchase price and the cost of repairs into one loan. So if you fall in love with a fixer-upper on a few acres outside of town, you may still have a path to buying it.

## Frequently Asked Questions About FHA Loans in Gilmer TX

**Can I use an FHA loan to buy land in Gilmer?**
Not on its own. FHA loans require a home to be on the property. You can buy land that already has a home on it, but you cannot use an FHA loan for raw land alone.

**Do I need perfect credit to get an FHA loan?**
No. FHA loans are designed for buyers who do not have perfect credit. A score of 580 or higher gets you the low down payment option. Scores between 500 and 579 may still work with a larger down payment.

**Are there income limits for FHA loans?**
No. There are no income limits. You can earn as much or as little as you want and still qualify, as long as you meet the credit and debt requirements.

**What if the home I want needs repairs?**
If the home needs significant work, a standard FHA loan might not pass the appraisal. The FHA 203k loan is worth asking about. It lets you finance the purchase price and the cost of repairs together in one loan.

**How long does it take to close on an FHA loan?**
Most FHA loans close in 30 to 45 days. The timeline depends on how quickly you gather your documents, how busy the lender is, and how smoothly the appraisal goes.
