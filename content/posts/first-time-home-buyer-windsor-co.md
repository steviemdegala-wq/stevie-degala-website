## What Loan Programs Work Best for First Time Buyers

You do not have to put 20 percent down to buy a home. There are several loan types that make buying more accessible.

**FHA Loans**

FHA loans are backed by the federal government. They let you put down as little as 3.5 percent, and they accept lower credit scores than most conventional loans. You will pay mortgage insurance every month, which is an extra fee that protects the lender. But many first time buyers use FHA because it gets them into a home sooner.

**Conventional Loans**

A conventional loan is not government backed. It usually requires better credit and a slightly higher down payment. But if you put down 20 percent, you skip mortgage insurance entirely. That saves real money over the life of the loan.

**USDA Loans**

Some areas near Windsor may qualify for USDA loans. These are designed for suburban and rural buyers. If the neighborhood qualifies and you meet income limits, you could buy with zero down.

**Down Payment Assistance**

Colorado's CHFA program, which stands for Colorado Housing and Finance Authority, offers grants and reduced rate loans for first time buyers who meet income and purchase price limits. Ask your lender about these because they can lower how much cash you need on closing day.

## First Time Home Buyer in Windsor CO: Steps to Get Ready

Getting pre approved is the most important thing you can do before you start touring homes. Pre approval means a lender has reviewed your income, credit, and debts and told you how much you qualify to borrow. It gives you a clear budget and shows sellers you are serious.

Here is a simple order to follow.

Check your credit first. Your score affects your rate and what programs you qualify for. Even small improvements can save you money every month.

Then figure out your full monthly budget. Your payment includes principal, interest, property taxes, homeowner's insurance, and sometimes HOA dues. Look at all of those together.

Save for more than just the down payment. Closing costs usually run between 2 percent and 5 percent of the loan amount. Some programs let sellers cover part of that, but you want cash ready either way.

Then find a lender you trust and get pre approved. A local mortgage broker shops multiple lenders for you instead of locking you into one bank's products. That often means a better rate and more flexibility.

## Common Mistakes First Time Buyers Make

Waiting too long is one of the most common. Buyers who wait for the perfect market often end up paying more. If your finances are in order, the right time is usually sooner than you think.

Skipping pre approval is another one. Shopping without knowing your budget wastes time and leads to disappointment. Do this step first.

Forgetting to budget for closing costs catches a lot of first time buyers off guard. Your lender is required to give you a Loan Estimate that breaks down all the costs, so review that carefully.

## FAQ: First Time Home Buyers in Windsor CO

**How much do I need to put down?**

It depends on the loan. FHA requires 3.5 percent. Some conventional loans go as low as 3 percent. USDA loans may allow zero down if you qualify. Down payment assistance can also help reduce what you need at closing.

**What credit score do I need?**

FHA loans work with scores as low as 580 in many cases. Conventional loans typically want 620 or higher. The higher your score, the better your interest rate will be.

**What is the difference between a bank and a mortgage broker?**

A bank only offers its own loan products. A mortgage broker like Stevie De Gala shops your loan across multiple lenders to find the best rate and terms for your situation. That gives you more options.

**Are there Colorado programs that help first time buyers?**

Yes. CHFA offers down payment assistance and reduced rate loans for buyers who meet income and purchase price limits. Ask your broker whether you qualify.

**How long does the process take from pre approval to closing?**

Once you have an accepted offer, closing usually takes 30 to 45 days. Getting pre approved before you shop speeds things up considerably.
