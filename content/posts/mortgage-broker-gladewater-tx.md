## Loan Programs Available to Gladewater Home Buyers

Every buyer's situation is a little different. Here are some of the most common loan types Gladewater buyers use.

**FHA Loans** are backed by the federal government and require as little as 3.5% down. They are a great fit if your credit score is still building or if you want to keep more cash in your pocket after closing.

**Conventional Loans** follow guidelines set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac. They often come with better rates if you have strong credit and can put at least 5% to 20% down.

**VA Loans** are available to veterans, active duty service members, and surviving spouses. They require no down payment and no private mortgage insurance. If you qualify, this is often the best deal available.

**USDA Loans** are worth knowing about if you are buying in a rural area. Parts of Upshur County and areas around Gladewater may qualify for USDA financing, which also requires no down payment.

**DSCR Loans** stand for Debt Service Coverage Ratio loans. They are designed for investors who want to buy rental property. Instead of using your personal income to qualify, the lender looks at the rental income the property will generate.

## FAQ: Mortgage Questions From Gladewater Buyers

**Do I need a big down payment to buy a home in Gladewater TX?**
Not necessarily. FHA loans allow as little as 3.5% down. VA loans and USDA loans require no down payment at all. Conventional loans can also work with as little as 3% down in some cases. Your best option depends on your credit and financial situation.

**What credit score do I need to get approved?**
It depends on the loan type. FHA loans generally work with scores as low as 580. Conventional loans typically want a score of at least 620, and better scores get you better rates. Even if your credit is not perfect, it is worth a conversation to see what is possible.

**Are rural properties harder to finance?**
They can be, depending on the condition of the property. Some loan programs have property requirements, especially around water systems and structural condition. A mortgage broker who knows rural lending can help you figure out which loan fits a specific property before you make an offer.

**How long does the mortgage process take?**
Most purchases close in 21 to 45 days once you are under contract. The timeline depends on the loan type, the lender, and how quickly everyone provides their paperwork. Getting pre approved before you shop speeds things up significantly.

**Can I use a USDA loan in the Gladewater area?**
Possibly, yes. Eligibility depends on the specific address and your household income. USDA loans are designed for rural and suburban areas, and parts of the Gladewater region qualify. Ask Stevie to check eligibility for any property you are considering.
