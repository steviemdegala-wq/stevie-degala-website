If you are house hunting in Timnath, you have probably heard the word "conventional" thrown around a lot. Conventional loans in Timnath, CO are simply home loans that are not backed by a government agency like the FHA or VA. They are the most common type of mortgage in the country, and they might be the best fit for you if you have steady income, a decent credit score, and some savings set aside. Let's walk through what that actually means for you and your new home.

## What Makes a Conventional Loan Different

A conventional loan is funded through a private lender and usually follows guidelines set by Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac, two organizations that buy and resell mortgages to keep money flowing to lenders. Because there is no government agency insuring the loan, lenders look closely at your credit score, your income, and your debt before approving you. In exchange, you often get more flexibility on the type of home you buy and how much you put down.

## Down Payments Can Be Lower Than You Think

A lot of people assume conventional loans always require 20% down. That is not true anymore. Many lenders, including the ones I work with, offer conventional loans with as little as 3% down for qualified first time buyers. If your down payment is under 20%, you will likely pay for private mortgage insurance, often called PMI, which protects the lender if you stop making payments. The good news is PMI on a conventional loan usually drops off once you reach 20% equity, unlike some other loan types where the insurance sticks around for the life of the loan.

## Credit Score and Income Matter Here

Because conventional loans are not government insured, lenders want to see a credit score that shows you handle debt responsibly, often in the 620 range or higher to qualify, with better rates available the higher your score climbs. Your income and your debt to income ratio, which is just your monthly debt payments compared to your monthly income, also play a big role. If your finances are in solid shape, a conventional loan can come with a lower interest rate than you might expect.
