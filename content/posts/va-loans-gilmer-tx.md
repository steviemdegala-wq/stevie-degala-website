## What Makes a VA Loan Different

A VA loan is backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. That backing gives lenders the confidence to offer you terms that are hard to find anywhere else.

No down payment required. Most home loans ask you to put money down, sometimes 3%, sometimes 20%. With a VA loan, you can finance the full purchase price of the home. That means you can stop renting and start building equity without draining your savings first.

No private mortgage insurance. Most loans with a low down payment require you to pay PMI, which is a monthly fee that protects the lender if you can't make your payments. VA loans do not require PMI, which can save you hundreds of dollars every month.

Competitive interest rates. Because the VA backs the loan, lenders can offer lower rates than you'd typically see on a conventional loan. Lower rates mean lower monthly payments and less interest paid over time.

## Who Qualifies for VA Loans in Gilmer, TX

You may qualify if you meet one or more of these service requirements. Veterans who served at least 90 consecutive days during wartime, or 181 days during peacetime, are typically eligible. Active duty service members who have served at least 90 continuous days also qualify. National Guard and Reserve members may qualify after six years of service. And surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service connected disability may be eligible as well.

You'll need a Certificate of Eligibility, which your lender can often help you obtain. You'll also need to meet credit and income requirements, though VA loans tend to be more flexible than other loan programs.

## Using Your VA Loan Benefit More Than Once

Some veterans think their VA loan benefit is a one time thing. It's not. You can use your VA loan benefit multiple times. If you've paid off a previous VA loan, your full entitlement is restored. And in many cases, even if you still have an active VA loan, you may have remaining entitlement to use on a second property.

This is especially useful if you're relocating to Gilmer and need to buy before you sell your current home. Talk to a local mortgage broker who understands VA loan entitlement and can map out your options clearly.

## FAQ: VA Loans in Gilmer, TX

**Is Gilmer, TX an affordable place to use a VA loan?**

Yes. Home prices in Gilmer and Upshur County tend to be well below the Texas average, which means your VA purchasing power goes a long way here. You can find solid single family homes without stretching your budget.

**Do I need perfect credit to get a VA loan?**

No. VA loans are more flexible on credit than conventional loans. While individual lenders set their own minimums, many will work with scores in the mid 600s. If your credit needs work, a good lender can also point you in the right direction before you apply.

**Is there a down payment required for a VA loan?**

No down payment is required for most VA loans. If you have full entitlement, you can finance 100% of the purchase price with no money down.

**Can I use a VA loan to buy land or a fixer upper in Gilmer?**

A VA loan can be used to purchase a home on land, but it cannot be used to buy raw land alone. For fixer uppers, there is a VA renovation loan option that may allow you to finance repairs. Ask your lender for details.

**What is the VA funding fee?**

The VA funding fee is a one time fee paid at closing that helps keep the VA loan program running. The amount depends on your service status, down payment, and whether it's your first time using the benefit. Many veterans with service connected disabilities are exempt from this fee entirely.
