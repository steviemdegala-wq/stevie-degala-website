## What a VA Loan Actually Is

A VA loan is a mortgage backed by the Department of Veterans Affairs. The VA does not lend you the money directly. Instead, they guarantee part of the loan, which means your lender takes on less risk and can offer you better terms. Most VA loans let you buy a home with zero down, and you skip PMI, which is the private mortgage insurance that other buyers often pay when they put down less than 20%. You do pay a one time funding fee in most cases, but that fee can be rolled into your loan instead of paid up front.

## Who Qualifies for a VA Loan

You generally qualify if you served on active duty, served in the National Guard or Reserves for a certain length of time, or you are the surviving spouse of a veteran who died in service or from a service connected condition. You will need a Certificate of Eligibility, often called a COE, which shows your lender that you meet the service requirements. ## Credit and Income Still Matter

A VA loan does not require a down payment, but lenders still look at your credit score and your income to make sure the payment fits your budget. There is no official minimum credit score set by the VA, but most lenders, including the ones I work with, like to see a score in the 580 to 620 range or higher. Your debt to income ratio, which compares your monthly debts to your monthly income, also plays a role. The good news is VA guidelines tend to be more flexible than conventional loans in this area.

## VA Loan Limits and Funding Fees

For most veterans with full entitlement, there is no official cap on how much you can borrow with a VA loan, as long as your income supports the payment and the lender approves the amount. The funding fee varies based on whether this is your first VA loan, your down payment amount, and your service history. Veterans with a service connected disability are often exempt from the funding fee entirely. Since these numbers change, I always confirm your exact fee and entitlement before we move forward.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I really not need a down payment for a VA loan in Windsor?**
That is correct for most eligible veterans. Many VA loans close with zero down, though you can choose to put money down if you want a lower payment.

**Is there a minimum credit score for a VA loan?**
The VA does not set one, but most lenders look for a score around 580 to 620 or higher. We can review your credit together to see where you stand.

**Can I use a VA loan more than once?**
Yes. VA loan benefits can be used multiple times throughout your life, as long as you have remaining entitlement and meet the requirements each time.

**What is the VA funding fee?**
It is a one time fee that helps keep the VA loan program running. The amount depends on your down payment and service history, and some veterans with a disability rating are exempt.

**Are VA loans only for first time buyers in Windsor?**
No. VA loans can be used for first time buyers, repeat buyers, and even refinances, including the streamlined VA refinance option for current VA loan holders.
