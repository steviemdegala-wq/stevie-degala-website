## Loan Programs Built for First Time Home Buyers in Longview TX

There is no single loan called a "first time home buyer loan." What actually exists are several programs that work especially well for people buying their first home.

FHA loans are one of the most popular starting points. FHA stands for Federal Housing Administration, which is the government agency that backs these loans. FHA loans let you put as little as 3.5% down, and they accept lower credit scores than conventional loans. If your credit score is at least 580, you can qualify for the 3.5% down option.

Conventional loans with 3% down are another option. These are not government backed, but they do allow small down payments if your credit is strong, usually 620 or higher.

USDA loans are worth mentioning too. If the home you are buying is in a rural or suburban area on the outskirts of Longview, you might qualify for a USDA loan, which allows zero down payment for eligible buyers in eligible locations.

A local mortgage broker can pull up all of these options and show you side by side what each one costs per month, so you can pick the one that fits your life.

## How Much Money Do You Actually Need to Buy in Longview TX

This is the question almost every first time buyer asks first, and the answer might surprise you.

You do not always need 20% down. In fact, most first time buyers in Longview put down far less than that.

Here is a rough breakdown of what you might need up front.

Down payment: as low as 3% to 3.5% of the purchase price, depending on the loan type you choose.

Closing costs: typically 2% to 4% of the loan amount. These cover things like the appraisal fee, title insurance, and lender fees. Some sellers will agree to pay a portion of your closing costs as part of the purchase agreement.

Earnest money: a small deposit, often $500 to $1,000 in this market, that you put down when you make an offer. This amount counts toward your down payment at closing.

On a $200,000 home in Longview, you might need somewhere in the range of $10,000 to $15,000 to close, depending on your loan type and whether the seller helps with costs.

## Down Payment Assistance Programs Available in East Texas

This is one of the most underused tools for first time home buyers, and a lot of people do not even know it exists.

There are programs through the Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation, often called TSAHC, and the Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs, called TDHCA, that offer grants and low interest second loans to help cover your down payment and closing costs.

Some of these programs are grant based, meaning you do not have to pay the money back. Others are structured as a second loan that gets forgiven over time as long as you stay in the home.

These programs have income limits and purchase price limits, but in Longview they often apply to a wide range of buyers. Your mortgage broker can check your eligibility in just a few minutes.

## Getting Pre Approved Before You Shop

Pre approval is the step you want to take before you start seriously looking at homes. A pre approval letter is a written statement from a lender that says they have reviewed your income, your credit, and your savings, and they are willing to lend you up to a certain amount.

Having pre approval does two things for you. First, it tells you exactly what price range to shop in so you are not falling in love with homes you cannot afford. Second, it shows sellers that you are a serious buyer. In any competitive market, a seller will almost always choose a buyer with pre approval over one without it.

Pre approval typically takes one to two business days and requires documents like your last two pay stubs, your last two years of tax returns, and your bank statements from the last two months.

## FAQ: First Time Home Buyers in Longview TX

**Do I need perfect credit to buy a home in Longview TX?**
No. FHA loans are available with a credit score as low as 580. Some programs work with scores a bit lower, though your options narrow and your rate may be higher. If your credit needs work, a good mortgage broker can help you build a plan before you apply.

**Can I get help with my down payment in Gregg County?**
Yes. Texas has several state level programs that offer down payment assistance, and some are available to buyers in Longview and Gregg County. These programs have income and purchase price limits, so the best way to find out if you qualify is to ask your broker directly.

**How long does it take to close on a home in East Texas?**
Most purchases close in 30 to 45 days from the time your offer is accepted. The timeline depends on how quickly you gather your documents, how fast the appraiser can schedule a visit, and whether the title search turns up any issues.

**What if I am self employed? Can I still get a mortgage?**
Yes, self employed buyers can absolutely get approved. You will typically need two years of tax returns and a profit and loss statement. There are also loan programs called bank statement loans that use your average monthly deposits instead of tax returns to verify your income.

**Is it better to rent or buy in Longview right now?**
In most cases in Longview, buying makes more financial sense over the long run. Rent does not build equity. A mortgage payment, on the other hand, builds ownership in your home a little more each month. With Longview home prices where they are, monthly mortgage payments are often comparable to rent, especially when you factor in down payment assistance.
