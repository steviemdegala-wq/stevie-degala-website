## What Makes VA Loans in Fort Collins CO Stand Out

A VA loan is a home loan backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. Lenders take on less risk because the government guarantees a portion of the loan. That guarantee turns into real benefits for you.

No down payment. You can buy a home in Fort Collins without putting any money down. That lets you keep your savings for moving costs, home repairs, or anything else that comes up.

No private mortgage insurance. Unlike FHA loans or conventional loans with less than 20 percent down, VA loans do not add that monthly PMI fee. Over the life of a loan, that adds up to thousands of dollars in savings.

Competitive rates. Because lenders carry less risk, they often offer lower interest rates on VA loans than on conventional loans.

More flexible credit standards. VA loans tend to be more forgiving of past credit challenges. You do not need a perfect credit history to qualify.

## Who Qualifies for a VA Loan

To use a VA loan, you need to meet military service requirements. You generally qualify if you served at least 90 consecutive days on active duty during wartime, 181 days during peacetime, or six years in the National Guard or Reserves. Surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service related disability may also be eligible.

You will need a Certificate of Eligibility, known as a COE, to confirm your benefit. Your lender can usually pull this for you directly through the VA system. It is a straightforward step.

Beyond your military record, lenders will also look at your income, your debt to income ratio (how much of your monthly income goes toward existing debts), and your credit score. Most VA lenders look for a score of 580 or higher, though some prefer 620 or above.

## What the VA Loan Process Looks Like

The VA loan process in Fort Collins follows a familiar path with a few extra steps specific to the program.

You start with preapproval. Your lender reviews your income, credit, and COE to determine how much you can borrow. That gives you a real number to work with when you start touring homes.

Once you find a home and the seller accepts your offer, the VA requires a home appraisal. A VA certified appraiser checks both the value of the property and whether it meets the VA's minimum property requirements. These requirements are there to protect you. They confirm the home is safe, sound, and ready to move into.

After the appraisal, your file goes through underwriting. Once everything checks out, you move to closing.

Most VA loans close in 30 to 45 days. Working with a lender who knows the VA loan process well can help things move faster.

## Understanding the VA Funding Fee

VA loans do not require private mortgage insurance, but they do include a one time VA funding fee. This fee goes back to the VA to keep the program funded for future veterans.

The amount depends on your down payment and whether you have used your VA benefit before. For most veterans using the benefit for the first time with no down payment, the fee is 2.15 percent of the loan amount. You can roll this into your loan so you do not have to bring extra money to closing.

Some veterans do not pay the funding fee at all. If you receive VA disability compensation, you are likely exempt. Your lender can verify this early in the process.

## FAQ: VA Loans Fort Collins CO

**Can I use a VA loan to buy a home in Fort Collins?**
Yes. VA loans work for any primary residence purchase in Fort Collins and throughout Larimer County. The home just needs to meet the VA's minimum property standards.

**Do I need a down payment?**
No. VA loans allow you to buy with zero down. You can choose to put money down if you want a lower monthly payment, but it is never required.

**What is the VA funding fee?**
It is a one time fee that helps fund the VA loan program. Most veterans using the benefit for the first time pay around 2.15 percent of the loan amount. Veterans receiving disability compensation are often exempt.

**Can I use my VA loan benefit more than once?**
Yes! As long as you have remaining entitlement or you have paid off a prior VA loan, you can use the benefit again. Many veterans use it multiple times over their lifetime.

**How long does the VA loan process take?**
Most VA loans in Fort Collins close in 30 to 45 days. The timeline can be shorter when you work with a lender who is experienced with VA loans and has a smooth process in place.
