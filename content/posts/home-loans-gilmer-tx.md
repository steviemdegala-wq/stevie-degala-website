## Loan Options to Know About

There is no single right loan for every buyer. Here is a quick look at the most common programs and who they tend to work best for.

**FHA Loans**

An FHA loan is backed by the federal government and designed to make buying more accessible. You can put down as little as 3.5 percent of the purchase price, and you do not need perfect credit to qualify. A score of 580 or higher is usually enough to get started. FHA loans are a popular choice for first time buyers here in Gilmer because they lower the barrier to entry.

**Conventional Loans**

A conventional loan is not backed by the government, so lenders set their own standards. Generally you want a credit score of 620 or above. If you can put 20 percent down, you avoid private mortgage insurance, which is the extra monthly fee lenders charge when your down payment is smaller. Conventional loans are a strong fit if you have solid credit and some savings built up.

**VA Loans**

If you served in the military, a VA loan is one of the best deals in home financing. You can buy with zero down payment and no private mortgage insurance. That saves you real money every month. Many veterans in Upshur County use this benefit, and if you qualify, it is worth exploring first.

**USDA Loans**

Parts of Upshur County qualify for USDA rural development loans. This program also allows zero down payment for buyers who meet income limits. If the home you are looking at is in a qualifying area, this could open a door that otherwise might feel closed.

## How to Get Ready Before You Apply

Getting prepared does not have to be complicated. A few simple steps can put you in a much stronger position when you apply.

**Review Your Credit**

Your credit score plays a big role in which loan you qualify for and what interest rate you get. You can pull your free report at annualcreditreport.com. Check for any errors and get them corrected early. Even a small boost in your score can lower your monthly payment.

**Save for Closing Costs**

Even low down payment loans come with closing costs. These include things like the appraisal, title insurance, and lender fees. Expect them to run between 2 and 5 percent of the loan amount. Knowing this upfront helps you plan your savings goals more accurately.

**Gather Your Documents Early**

Lenders will ask for recent pay stubs, two years of tax returns, bank statements, and a photo ID. If you are self employed, you will also need a profit and loss statement. Having these ready before you apply saves time and keeps things moving.

**Get Pre Approved**

A pre approval letter shows sellers you are a serious buyer. It means a lender has already reviewed your finances and is ready to work with you. In a market like Gilmer where a good home can go quickly, that letter gives you an edge.

## FAQ: Home Loans in Gilmer TX

**What credit score do I need to get a home loan in Gilmer TX?**

It depends on the loan type. FHA loans typically accept scores of 580 or above with a 3.5 percent down payment. Conventional loans usually require a 620 or higher. VA and USDA loans have their own guidelines. The best way to know where you stand is to have a quick conversation with a lender.

**Can I buy a home with land in Gilmer using a standard home loan?**

Yes, in many cases. If the property is primarily residential and the land is not too large, most loan programs will apply. Properties with significant acreage or those classified as farms may need a different approach. Your lender will review the property details during underwriting and let you know.

**How long does the home loan process take?**

Most purchases close in 30 to 45 days from the time your offer is accepted. Getting pre approved before you start shopping helps speed things up because your finances are already reviewed.

**Are there down payment assistance programs available in Gilmer TX?**

Yes. Texas offers programs through TDHCA, the Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs, that help with down payment and closing costs. Ask your mortgage broker which programs you qualify for based on your income and the purchase price.

**What is the difference between a mortgage broker and a regular bank?**

A bank offers its own loan products only. A mortgage broker works with many lenders and finds the best match for you. Brokers often find better rates and terms because they have access to more options.
